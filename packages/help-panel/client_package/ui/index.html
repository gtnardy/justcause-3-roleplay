Getting Started

Getting Started - First Job, Leveling Up and Earning Money

In Just Cause - Roleplay, you can carry out Jobs alongside players from around the world. As you play you will gain Experience (XP). Jobs have tasks that provide extra XP. As your XP increases, your Level goes Up. This will unlock new Jobs, equipment and abilities. Cash is another key to success in Just Cause - Roleplay: buy Houses, Licenses for Driving, Utensils and entertain yourself and others with various activities.

New players with low level are benefited with bonus experience and are dispensed with some mechanics until they become accustomed to the game.

Until level 5: You'll receive +50% bonus experience for doing jobs and you won't have to pay hospital expenses.
Until level 10: You won't be punished for driving without license.

Leveling Up and Earning Money

Leveling Up and Earning Money is the basic of any action on the server! By doing Jobs, you earn money and experience. The money you get by doing jobs will be given to your pocket, so don't forget to deposit it into the Bank. The experience will fill the bar at the top right side of your screen. When it's completely filled, you will level up, unlocking new Jobs and things to do. Aside from doing jobs, you will get extra experience every twenty minutes, and your salary every hour (deposited automatically into your bank account).

If you are AFK, you will not receive the extra XP nor the salary.

 Getting your first Job

When you join the server for the first time, you are Unemployed, and are receiving from the government an aid of $600/hour. So the first step is to look for a Town Hall to get your first job (if you need, use your GPS by pressing M (Interaction Menu) to find it).

You can interact with Menus by using Arrow Keys ↑ ↓ ← ↲.

Once you are in Town Hall, access the Jobs menu to see all available jobs. Here, you will be able to see a brief description of each job, as well as the hourly salary. As a new player you will only have Hunter and Farmer to choose, we recommend testing both to see which one you like best.

Remembering that in addition to the salary, you get paid for actively working as well. See Jobs section for more details about Jobs

When you choose a job, make sure to choose to teleport to the job spawn position, after this you will be able to see a new menu at the M Interaction Menu with specific explanations and commands of your new job.

You can always change your job at any time, just look for a Town Hall. It doesn't stop there! We recommend you continuing to enjoy this guide for full server knowledge. Read more about Useful Commands, Jobs and Locations.
You can also always count with us to answer any question in our Social Channels.

Necessities - Hunger and Thirst

Over time, you will get hungry and thirsty. You can see the current status at the bottom right of your screen, if you go hungry/thirst for a long time, you may die.

To fill your necessities up, you need to find a Snackbar. In Médici you will find several unique Snackbars, with their own foods and drinks to offer, be sure to try them all! The foods/drinks give different levels of satiety!

Attention: some foods can make you thirsty!

AFK System

If you are inactive for some minutes, you will get AFK. In this state you will not receive the extra experience every twenty minutes, neither the salary every hour. You can circumvent this system by donating to the server and becoming a VIP, so you'll not be penalized with the ANTI-AFK sytem.

To leave the AFK-state, type /afk [the code]. The code will be randomly generated and displayed on your screen.

Fuel & Gas Station

Driving vehicles spends fuel. You can find your fuel bar at the bottom right side of your screen. Keep in mind that it is only visible in cars. If you need to refuel, just visit a Gas Station and choose on how much you want to fuel. The fuel you have, is for all vehicles. If you run out of fuel completely, you can not enter any vehicle anymore (until you refuel again), so always watch the fuel bar and fuel in time!

Not any vehicle has it´s own fuel tank. So if you run out of fuel, another player can still use the vehicle.

Each vehicle has a different fuel expense, for example: motorcycles and light cars spend less fuel than trucks and airplanes.

Jobs

In Just Cause - Roleplay you can work in the most diverse jobs available. The Jobs on the server are listed under three different factions: Civilian Jobs, Military Jobs, and Rebel Jobs. Changing jobs is only possible at the Town Hall and if you have the minimum level required.

Every job pays a salary every hour of playing.

Most jobs are 'action jobs', so you have to work actively and do certain actions to get the major XP and Money. The other miority are just 'fun jobs' that don't have active actions to make money and XP.

When switching to a new Job, press M (Interaction Menu) and open your Job menu to see their description and all available commands.

Town Hall

You can change your current Job at any time, for that you only need to go to one Town Hall and see the list of available jobs for your actual level. When you accept a new Job, you are able to choose to teleport to the Job Spawn Point or stay at the Town Hall. Jobs are locked behind a certain level. at level 1, you can choose to be a Farmer or Hunter. The next job unlocks at level 3.

Each Job will deploy you at a different Spawn Point.

Wanted Stars

The Wanted Stars in Just Cause - Roleplay is an indicator of how many crimes you have committed. Each crime you commit you'll get a certain quantity of Stars, that will be alerted right away. Some crimes are caught by the server automatically, and you get wanted automatically too. For other crimes, players have to denounce (/911) the criminal so he gets wanted.

Some Wanted Stars disappear as time passes, so the only thing you should do is hide from the police.

Police

You can get rid of your Stars only in two ways: Being arrested or Paying for them. If you are being wanted, and a police officer approach and inspect you, he will see all your crimes. If the quantity of Stars is greater than or equal to 2, he can handcuff you and take you to the Police Department, then arrest you.

Each type of crime has a specific amount of stars assigned, a value to be paid and a time of arrest, all of which will accrue if you pay or be arrested.

Police Department

Other way to get rid of the Wanted Stars is to pay for them, you can do that at the Police Department. At the Police Department, besides being able to pay for your Stars, you can buy Weapon Carry Permit and you can see all your crimes that you committed in detail, and their respective values.

Paying for Wanted Star is less painful as you do not have to be stuck at the jail for a while, other than paying for them you will get a 25% discount. When you are arrested, in addition to being stuck for a while, you will also pay the full value for each star, automatically.

Bank

As you work, you'll get rewarded with money, that will be stored at your pocket. If you die, you'll lose part of your money in your pocket (see Hospital for details). So the most safety thing to do is store all your money into the Bank.

At the bank, you can see your Balance, as you can Withdraw or Deposit money there.

Savings

The bank has a saving of 0.2% per hour, and if you're VIP, it'll yield at a rate of 0.4%/hour. So every money you let there will save 0.2% per played hour.

Hospital If you are injured, you can look for a hospital, or a paramedic. In the hospital, in addition to being able to heal yourself, you can buy Life Insurance and Health Insurance.

When you die, you lose some of the money in your pocket, in addition to having to pay the expenses of the hospital, to avoid it, you can buy the Hospital Insurances:

Life Insurance: will refund the money lost from your pocket when you die.
Health Insurance: will cover hospital expenses when you die.

New players are not charged for hospital expenses, but should try to buy the insurances as fast as possible.

GPS & Locations

You can find the GPS in the Interaction Menu that you can open by pressing the M. It is the first option listed there.

Choose the location you want to go to by using the Arrow Keys ↑ ↓ ←, and press Enter ↲ to select.

A waypoint will be put onto your map and you will be able to see it by looking around as well. If you set the GPS to snackbar for example, the waypoint will be placed onto the nearest snackbar. The same goes for any other installation that exists more than once. As a result of limitations to server-owners, the markers set by the GPS can not show arrows on the road as the player placed ones do.

Pressing “Clear GPS” will delete the current waypoint.

Military Bases

Military Bases are the core of the economy of Just Cause Roleplay. All bases Have a Bavarium Storehouse and a Bavarium Processing Plant.

Each hour, the Bavarium Processing Plant of each Military Base will process 50 Bavarium (if stock exists), and they will be sold for the current price of Bavarium (as can be seen in the Newspaper).

The bases are in constant dispute between the Military and the Rebels, both trying to take control of all bases.

If the base is owned by the government, the money obtained by processing the Bavarium goes to the government banks, otherwise, there is still no functionality for the Rebels.

Conquering and Defending a Military Base

If you are a Rebel Mercenary or a Military Soldier, you are able to conquer bases to your faction. The system is like famous games of base conquest (like Battlefield - Conquest mode): you go to base and begin the conquest by hoisting your flag.

When entering a base, all players of the opposing faction will be notified that there is enemy activity on the Base that you are.

After being captured, the Bases will be protected for a period of 12 hours, in which they cannot be taken by the opposing side. The same happens if you can defend a base, but in this case it will be protected for less time.

All bases are supplied with Military Vehicles that can only be used by the faction that dominates that base in specific, if the base is dominated, all the vehicles are switched for the respective of the faction that dominates it now.

Note: in all military bases, the PVP is allowed, ie: you will not be arrested or wanted by the police if you kill other players there. So it's permitted to confront players in these areas (which makes the job of truck drivers a bit dangerous).

If you're a Military or Rebel you can see all the bases flags at the top of the screen.

Supplying the Bavarium Storehouse

For now the bases can only be supplied by the Truck Driver. This one will load your Truck with Bavarium at the harbor and will be assigned to any Government-owned Base that are lacking in Bavarium, to be restocked.

Each delivery fuels the base with 1000 Bavarium.

Gun Store

In Just Cause - Roleplay, the players are able to buy legal Weapons at the Gun Store, for that is necessary to possess the Weapon Carry Permit, which can be purchased at the Police Department.

There is several weapons in the Gun Store avaiable to purchase, however each weapon requires a minimum level to be unlocked for purchase.

Only VIP players can buy special bazookas.

Once purchased, the guns will be permanently available to equip, they have infinite ammunition and can be equipped either in the Gun Store or in the Weapon Rack of your House.

Convenience Store The Convenience Store is a shop where you can buy useful items that you cannot get anywhere else. One of these items is the parachute. The other one is the Wingsuit (and upgrades), which is only available for VIPs.
Driving School

Licenses are needed to get access to different kinds of vehicles. If you are under level 10, you can drive cars and bikes freely. Once you reach level 10, you will need the license for Cars and Bikes to be able to legally drive them. If you drive without a license, you will become Wanted and officers can arrest you if you do it twice and more times.

You can drive Cars and Bikes without license, but can't with Helicopters, Boats and Planes.

Races

Races are a type of game mode available in Just Cause - Roleplay. You can play alone or with up dozens of players. Races give you rewards such as Money and XP. For now there is only Land Races, which are divided into two different types (more types cooming soon): Circuit and Point to Point. Circuit races involve repeated timed laps around a set course and Point to Point races run from a start point to a separate finish point.

There are more than 70 unique player-created races at Just Cause - Roleplay.

We are working to release Air Races and Water Races soon.

Creating a Lobby

To start one Race Lobby, you will need to go to the Race Spot and choose to Open a Lobby. You'll be able to choose the following variables and the track:

Type: Land/Air/Sea
Laps: From 1-99 (not applicable to Point-to-Point races)
Vehicle Class: e.g. Sports, Off-Road, Supercars, Old...
Nitro: if the Nitrous is enabled
Race Bet: if the race will have a bet, all players automatically pay the value and the winner gets everything

Joining a Lobby

If you want to join a lobby, open the M Interaction Menu or go to a Race Spot, choose Race Lobbies and select the lobby you want to join. You'll be able to see all the variables of the race, even if it's with Bet.

Creating a Race Track

To be able to create a Race Track, you must be a Race Designer (see Jobs). Once you are a Race Designer, you are able to see a new option in your M Interaction Menu, under 'Race Designer' item: Create a new Track. Once submited, if your race is very good, it can become verified by the server .

Tips/Track Guidelines

It must have enough Starting Positions for players be able to join the race. If it's a Circuit Race, then the last Checkpoint should be close of the first. Make it be fun ;).
Remember to save your race from time to time so you don't have the risk of losing it for various reasons.

Over time, your stats will decrease, so keep practicing them to keep them going.

Collectibles

In Just Cause - Roleplay you can hunt for Collectibles and get XP and Money for that. There are 5 types of Collectibles to find: DiRavello Audio Tapes, Shrines, Treasures, Daredevil Jumps and Ancient Tombs.

Vehicles

As a socialist state, Medici offers vehicles to everyone for free. You can pick up then from anywhere on the roleplay island. In addition to this, player can buy their own Personal Car or Bike at Dealership that they can locate, lock, and respawn for $150 at their house after it gets destroyed.

Stealing a vehicle from another person who is driving at that moment is considered a crime, giving one wanted star to the thief. Players above level 9 need a license to drive cars and bikes, or it will be considered a crime. To enter a plane, helicopter or boat as driver, you need a license as well. Licenses can be bought at the Driving School.

Personal Vehicle

Players that own a house are allowed to buy personal vehicles.

When a vehicle is bought, is spawns at the parking lot at the shop and you can take it with you. In the interaction menu (M) you can find a personal vehicle menu. Here you can locate your vehicle - which sets a marker on it - and lock/ unlock it. You don't have to be in the vehicle to do this. A locked vehicle can't be entered from the driver side by other players. Your vehicle also has a plate (name) that is shown like a players nametag on the vehicle.

The moment you join the server, your personal vehicle spawns at its last position. It is also possible to spawn your car at your house in the menu of your house. If your vehicle is destroyed, you have to use this menu to trigger your car's insurance and spawn it back at your house for $150. When you spawn a personal vehicle at your house, your old vehicle will be parked in the garage.

Like any car, you can mod your cars at a Pay'n Spray or let a mechanic do it. Only at a pay'n spray you can edit the vehicles plate. Modifications (paint, nitro, turbo jump, plate) will be permanent, even after your vehicle is destroyed and you have to trigger the insurance, or when you join the server later. You can always change the plate and colour again.

When you buy a house you get the first garage slot for free. For $15.000 you can buy a second slot, and each new slot is $15.000 more expensive than the last one. Normal players can buy up to 10 garage slots, and VIP players can buy up to 20.

There are three Dealerships. Two for cars and one for bikes and quad bikes. To sell a personal vehicle, bring it to a pay'n spray.

Dealership

There are three dealerships where you can buy Personal Vehicles.

To buy a vehicle and use it, you need a House first. Only when you have spare garage slots you can buy a new vehicle. When you buy a vehicle, it will spawn on a parking lot close to the shop.
